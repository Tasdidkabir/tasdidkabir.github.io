CLIMATE CHANGE

A long-term shift in the typical weather patterns that have come to characterise local, regional, and global climates on Earth is referred to as climate change. Human activities, especially the burning of fossil fuels, which raises the concentrations of heat-trapping greenhouse gases in Earth's atmosphere and thus increases the planet's average surface temperature, are to blame for the climate changes that have been observed since the middle of the 20th century. Internal variability and external forcings are examples of natural processes that can cause climate change but have been outweighed by human activity. Global warming is not a future issue. Increased human emissions of heat-trapping greenhouse gases are altering the Earth's climate, which is already having a significant impact on the environment. Glaciers and ice sheets are melting, lake and river ice is breaking up earlier, plant and animal ranges are shifting, and plants and trees are blooming earlier. Here is a brief discussion of the causes of climate change and the importance in todays world.

Causes
The main cause of global warming which causes climate change is inflicted by human activity. Over the last century, burning of fossil fuels like coal and oil has increased the concentration of atmospheric carbon dioxide (CO2). The heat which is created by the sun, 90% of it gets absorbed by the atmosphere containing nitrous oxide, methane, chlorofluorocarbons and carbon dioxide and causes the atmosphere to heat up. Moreover, the industrial activities that our modern civilization depends upon have raised atmospheric carbon dioxide levels by nearly 50% since 1750, a great contributing factor on climate change. To a lesser extent, clearing of land for agriculture, industry, and other human activities has increased concentrations of greenhouse gases. Increase in population is also a huge contributing factor for climate change. As it stands, a growth in population results in a rise of energy consumption which leads to energy loss and creates more global warming. It also leads to soaring deforestation, exhausted argicultural resourses and drinking water. Even the amount of transportation and vehicle needs accumulates, one of the main source Carbon Dioxide(C02) release.

Importance
Impacts of climate change may be observed in every area of the world we live in. However, the effects of climate change are not uniform throughout the nation and the world; even within a single city, different neighbourhoods or people may experience different effects. The effects of climate change on many societal segments are interconnected. Food production and human health can be harmed by drought. Flooding has the potential to spread illness and harm infrastructure and ecosystems. Health problems can reduce worker productivity, raise mortality, and have an influence on the availability of food. Because of the rise of temperature, icebergs on the nothern parts of the world are melting and causing sea levels to rise at a quicker rate. By a few decades, large amount of lands will go underneath the ocean cause of the rise in sea levels. Moreover, climate change is already affecting human health. Hurricanes are becoming more intense and wetter as ocean temperatures increase, which can result in both direct and indirect fatalities. Flooding more often can increase the risk of chemical risks, accidents, and waterborne infections. The geographic ranges of ticks and mosquitoes are expanding, and they can spread illness to new areas. And if these serious consequences are not realizing and dealt with, there will be no point of return in the future.

In conclusion, global action is urgently needed to address the significant threat posed by climate change. Climate change will have an impact on people's access to water, the ability to produce food, their health, and the environment. As the world heats, hundreds of millions of people might experience famine, water shortages, and coastal floods. According to a review done upon former economic models, the entire costs and hazards of climate change will be comparable to losing at least 5% of global GDP year, starting now and forever, if we don't take action. The estimated harm might reach 20% of GDP or more if a wider range of hazards and effects are included. As a result, it has become very relevant to tackle this situation as soon as possible.
