Fourth-Gen Tensor Cores

The democratisation of computing for AI and HPC has been fueled by NVIDIA GPUs, whose peak performance has improved by 60X since the launch of Tensor Core technology. Fourth-generation Tensor Cores are advanced by the NVIDIA HopperTM architecture with the Transformer Engine, which offers 6X more performance than FP16 for training trillion-parameter models. Hopper Tensor Cores provide the biggest speedups to all workloads when combined with 3X higher performance using TF32, FP64, FP16, and INT8 precisions.


Shader Execution Reordering

A series of diverging, inefficient workloads for shaders are produced by advanced ray tracing, which entails computing the effects of many rays impacting several different material types throughout a picture (shaders calculate the appropriate levels of light, darkness, and colour during the rendering of a 3D scene, and are used in every modern game). These formerly inefficient workloads are dynamically reorganised into much more efficient ones using the Shader Execution Reordering (SER) technology. SER can increase in-game frame rates by up to 25% and shader performance for ray tracing tasks by up to 3X.


DLSS 3

By leveraging AI to produce higher resolution frames from lower resolution inputs, this feature improves performance for all GeForce RTX GPUs. In order to rebuild native quality pictures, DLSS samples a number of lower resolution images and incorporates motion information and feedback from earlier frames.
